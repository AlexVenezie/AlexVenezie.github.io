About

Connecting mechanics to medicine

I'm a PhD student at Washington University in St. Louis studying how mechanical forces translate into brain injury. My work sits at the intersection of engineering and neuroscience — using imaging and computational modeling to understand what happens to the brain at the moment of impact.

My research focuses on traumatic brain injury biomechanics, advanced MRI techniques, and developing tools that can better predict injury thresholds and outcomes. I believe that the path to better TBI prevention and treatment runs through a deeper understanding of the physics involved.
